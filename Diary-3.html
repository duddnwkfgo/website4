March 24th, 2018



I am listening to Haruki Murakami’s Norwegian Wood. At first, it was pretty lame. I guess I had a prejudice towards the novels Haruki Murakami wrote. I have heard= that it’s one of the best authors out there is in Japan and everyone who’ve told me about Haruki just couldn’t stop praising how good his books are. So I thought his books were philosophical, complicated filled with thought-provoking questions that he asks through his books. Turns out, it’s nothing like what I had imagined. His books – or at least Norweigan Wood which I am listening to at the moment – are simply written to the point that even preschoolers could understand. His sentences are simple and concise. And it’s quite hilarious. Time and again, I found myself chuckling listening to the book – with a little bit of pang of sadness. It’s hilarious because it’s so true (I could use some better vocabularies/description but, oh well) and it’s hurts. It’s funny because it’s true and fucked-up. For example, here’s the dialogue Midori and Watanabe had. Actually, I’ll paraphrase it. Midori said “I don’t have money” is a line that only rich people can say. It’s like the really ugly girls don’t say “I am not pretty” because it’s just too said to say and admit it. And I chuckled hard because I, too, say “I don’t have money” absent-mindly. It’s not a hard pill to shallow because I know myself too well that I have means to get money even though I may not have money on me at the moment. Who am I kidding, right? This is what one of my good friends criticize me for, saying that I am just doing a cosplay and that I have a choice to have or not have the money. Yes, I can and have chosen to not to be a full-time employee, simply because I didn’t like it; Yes, I have chosen minimum wage part-time job so that I can do whatever I want in the downtime –like writing this diary right now; and yes, I have the luxury of pursuing my own dream without any baggages. The world isn’t fair. Some people are more-or-less stuck with soul-sucking depressing jobs they hate because they can’t afford to take time off. They don’t have a choice – Or at least, they put tremendous amount of pressure on themselves to make the right choices. Some people can’t afford to make wrong decisions. Some people can’t afford to be a happy-go-lucky because they can’t afford to fail. Some people can say “LOL I don’t have money. Haha, I am so broke” while others are hearing the voice message of “Sorry, please wait for the next available assistant” on a suicide hotline. It’s funny cuz it’s true. So I couldn’t stop laughing hysterically.
